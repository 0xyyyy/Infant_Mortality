Scatter Plot

This graph is a "would-like-be" interactive scatter plot. As a default it shows the relationship between infant mortality and median income for each state. If you toggle through the axis labels, you can compare Teen birth rates, unmarried mother birth rates, and total infant deaths.

Key Takeaways

When looking at a state's median income, there is a clear trend showing a decline of infant deaths when the median income increases. While there is a variance between states with similar incomes, the main trend shows that for every $10,000 increase in a state's median income, the mortality rate decreases by about 1 per 1000 births.
